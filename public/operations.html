Operations Guidance

Step-by-step process reference for counselors.

What This App Does

This replaces paper logs, spreadsheets, and disconnected tracking. Everything lives in one place — client profiles, session history, financials, HUD time, billing, and reports.

Counseling Log
Central record of every session. Anyone can view and edit entries. Admins can delete.
Client Profiles
Full profile per client — identity, intake details, financials, session history, Rx numbers, and Drive folder.
My HUD Time
PAR log and CML tracking. NOFA In-Person sessions auto-import as Counseling entries — you only need to add non-session time manually.
Buyer Ready / Repair Ready
Ranked lists for pre-purchase and repair grant clients. Priority score auto-calculated by AMI, budget, and wait time.
Reports
CDBG monthly reports and court appearance summaries. Executive Director also has the HUD Reports tab to generate CAL, PAR, CML, TAL, INV, and full ZIP submissions.
Outreach
Log outreach events, track contacts, and record follow-up activity.
Transition Checklist — What Every Counselor Needs to Do

Complete these steps to get fully set up. Ongoing tasks are marked separately.

One-time setup
1
Create a client profile for every active client. Go to Counseling Log → open each client → a profile will exist if it was already created, or use "+ New Client" to create one. Fill in the Overview tab (name, counseling type, billing type, AMI, RE code) and the Intake tab (contact info, household).
2
Fill in the Financials tab for each active client. Enter their employment history, other income sources, monthly expenses, and liabilities. This data is captured in session snapshots going forward so the record stays current.
3
Link Rx numbers on the client's Overview tab. If a client has existing NOFA or HEMAP Rx numbers, add them under the Rx Numbers panel. Use the "→ Add" buttons to pull any Rx numbers already found in session history.
4
Link the client's Google Drive folder on the Overview tab. This keeps documents accessible directly from the profile.
5
Add any Buyer Ready clients to the Buyer Ready list and any POST repair clients to the Repair Ready list. Both pages have an "+ Add Client" button that pulls from existing profiles.
Every session (ongoing)
A
Log the session on the client's Sessions tab — date, hours, Rx number, case status, notes. This is the primary billing and activity record. The session automatically creates a snapshot of the client's current profile and financials at that point in time.
B
Log any non-session HUD time in My HUD Time — processing, billing, supervision, management, training, marketing. NOFA In-Person and Case Management sessions import automatically; only add entries for time that isn't tied to a specific session.
C
Keep client financials current. Update the Financials tab whenever income, expenses, or liabilities change. The next session snapshot will capture the updated figures.
Safely Home — Confidentiality Program

This program supports clients fleeing domestic violence or other safety threats. It is designed to comply with VAWA (Violence Against Women Act) confidentiality requirements and HUD's Continuum of Care privacy standards (24 CFR §578.103).

Standard
Normal program clients. Visible to all counselors and admins. Appears in all lists and reports.
Confidential
Visible only to the client's care team and the Executive Director. Appears in Buyer Ready and Repair Ready lists only if you are on the care team. Shown as "Confidential" to authorized viewers — never labeled as DV.
Protected
Same access controls as Confidential, plus the client is fully excluded from program waitlists. Used for the highest-risk clients where even list visibility is a concern. Shown as "Protected" to authorized viewers.
How it works
1
Enrollment: Select "Safely Home (Confidential)" from the Program dropdown when creating a new client. The enrolling counselor is automatically added as the initial care team member.
2
Care team management: The Executive Director can add or remove care team members from the Confidentiality section on the client's profile. Only the ED can change a client's tier or assign team members.
3
Visibility: Unauthorized users cannot see these clients in the client list, Buyer Ready, or Repair Ready. They receive no indication that the record exists. The client name and all data are completely hidden.
4
Audit log: Every access to a Confidential or Protected profile is recorded in the system audit log with the user name and timestamp. All tier changes are logged with the reason provided by the ED.
Legal basis & compliance
i
VAWA (Violence Against Women Act): Prohibits disclosure of personally identifying information about DV/SA victims served by HUD-funded programs without informed, written, time-limited consent. This system enforces that at the data access layer — non-team members cannot retrieve the record at all.
i
HUD CoC Privacy Rule (24 CFR §578.103): Requires that recipients collect only the minimum necessary information, establish procedures to ensure data security, and limit access to staff with a need to know. The care team model implements the "need to know" principle — access is restricted to the assigned team, not the entire organization.
i
What is never stored: The system does not store any label like "DV", "domestic violence", "Safely Home", or "restricted" in any field visible to unauthorized users. Authorized viewers see "Confidential" or "Protected." No one outside the care team sees anything.
i
HUD reporting: Confidential and Protected clients are included in aggregate HUD reports (counts, demographics) but are never identified individually in any report output. Address fields are excluded from any exported data for these clients.
Key Things to Know
i
Session snapshots are permanent. When a new session is saved, it captures the client's full profile and financials at that moment. Editing the session later does not overwrite the snapshot — it is a point-in-time record.
i
Rx numbers live on client profiles, not sessions. A single client may have multiple Rx numbers (NOFA, HEMAP, etc.) each with their own guarantor. Add them under the Rx Numbers panel on the Overview tab.
i
HUD Time auto-imports NOFA sessions. Any session logged for an In-Person client with an active NOFA Rx number will appear automatically in My HUD Time as a Counseling entry. You do not need to enter those manually.
i
Client profiles export to PDF. Use the "Export PDF" button on any client profile and select which tabs to include — Overview, Intake, Financials, and/or Sessions.
i
Appointment letters are generated here. Go to the Intake tab on this page, fill in the client details, check off the relevant documents, and click Print — a formatted confirmation letter opens ready to print or save as PDF.
Pre-Purchase Counseling — Buying Power

Buying Power is a buyer-advocacy approach to pre-purchase counseling. Its purpose is to help buyers win — not just learn. It does two things: make buyers objectively closing-ready based on real lender underwriting standards, and coordinate all eligible assistance — internal and external — so cash-to-close is minimized and deals don't fail late.

It is not referrals. It is not a preferred lender list. It is not generic education. It is transaction readiness plus capital coordination.

What makes it different: Most organizations stop at education. Buying Power engineers outcomes — pressure-testing readiness before clients shop, identifying deal killers early, and coordinating all assistance upfront so underwriting sees a clean file. We operate across all five phases of a purchase: Financial Reality, Lender Readiness, Offer Competitiveness, Underwriting Survival, and Closing Execution.

Where buyers lose: Pre-approval mistaken for readiness. Assistance discovered too late. Missing or inconsistent documents during underwriting. Unexpected cash-to-close at the table. Every one of these failure points is preventable.

"We are not here to send you somewhere. We are here to make you ready, funded, and hard to derail. That is how buyers win. That is all we are optimizing for."
